📝 Selected Publications

Obliviator Reveals the Cost of Nonlinear Guardedness in Concept Erasure
Ramin Akbari*, Milad Afshari*, Vishnu Boddeti
Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS), Dec 2025

Concept erasure aims to remove unwanted attributes from learned representations. Current methods fail to guard against nonlinear adversaries because they do not capture the statistical dependencies between unwanted concepts and learned representations. We introduce Obliviator, a method that captures nonlinear statistical dependencies to successfully guard against nonlinear adversaries. By using an iterative approach, Obliviator prevents mode-collapse and preserves as much information as possible during erasure, achieving a strong benchmark on the utility-erasure trade-off.
